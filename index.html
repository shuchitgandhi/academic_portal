A. Registration

1. What is registration procedure ?

The followings are necessary sequential steps to register for a semester:

a. The registration process is announced in advance.

b. Students need to fill the course registration form on the e-campus portal within the given time which will be informed to students via the webmail.

c. Students can further change their courses in the Add/Drop period in the beginning of each semester.

d. Late registration will be allowed for valid reasons on submission of an application to the deputy registrar, And only on payment of prescribed late registration fee. The amount of the late fee will be clearly articulated in the registration notice sent by the Deputy Registrar.

2. Is there any eligibility for course registration ?

All students with no backlogs courses (i.e. who have passed all previous courses) will be eligible to register for any course prescribed in the curriculum for that semester.

A student who is on probation or has any backlog course(s) may be recommended a different set of courses, by the convener of the UG committee.

3. Is there any pre-requisite criterion to register for any course?

A student registering for any course must have successfully completed the Pre-requisite course(s), if any, for that particular course. Students can ask the pre-requisite criterion directly from the faculty assigned for that course or can also find it in the “Courses” folder on the daiictpdc.

B. Couse-Structure

1. What are the different categories of elective courses offered in a particular semester?

There are four categories of elective courses offered during any semester.

2. What is the credit structure of a course ?

Every course has a particular credit structure known as LTPC structure.

Ex. a course with (3-0-2-4) credit structure has:

3 hours of lecture per week

2 hours of lab per week

Total credits of the course = 4

C. Course Load

1. Regular Semester

 a. What is the minimum number of courses and credits a student need to register in particular semester?

 A student has to register for minimum of 4 courses and 12 credits in any semester except in the eighth semester, in which he/she can take less than 4 courses.

 b. What is the maximum number of courses and credits a student need to register in particular semester?

 A student has to register for maximum of 6 courses and 26 credits in any semester except in the eighth semester, in which he/she cannot take more than 3 courses along with the B.Tech Project.

2. Summer Semester

 a. What is the maximum number of courses and credits a student need to register in summer semester?

 A student can register for maximum of 3 backlog courses in summer semester.

 For the purpose of this rule, internship will be regarded as two courses and B.Tech Project Part II will be regarded as 3 courses.

D. Audit Courses

1. What do you mean by an audit course ?

Auditing of the courses allow students to gain exposure to additional subjects without increasing their overall workload. Students can take audit courses after fifth semester onwards.

2. What are maximum numbers of audit a student can take ?

A student can take maximum of two courses during the entire B.Tech program.

3. How to register for an audit course ?

A student has to enter the course(s) to be audited in the Course Registration Form (CRF) while registering for the semester. The word “Audit” is specially mentioned in the remarks column of the student’s Course Registration Form.

4. Is/Are there any condition/s in order to be eligible to register for an audit course ?

A student can register a course for audit provided the following conditions are satisfied :

a. The course instructor permits and approves the registration, and

b. The lecture, lab and tutorial time table strictly permits.

5. How will be the course assessment done of an audit course ?

If the student’s performance is satisfactory, a grade of P (Pass) would be awarded. If the performance id not satisfactory, a grade of F(Fail) would be awarded.

6. Will an audit courses will be considered in calculation of Semester Point Index (SPI)/ Cumulative Point Index (CPI) ?

No.

However, the course will be reflected in Semester Grade Report and Transcript as an Audit course provided a grade of P was obtained, otherwise the grade will not appear in Semester Grade Report and Transcript.

E. Registration for PG-Level Courses

1. When can students register for PG level course(s) ?

Students can register for PG course(s) from sixth semester onwards.

2. Is/Are there any condition/s in order to be eligible to register for PG level courses ?

A student can register for PG level course provided the following three conditions are satisfied:

a. The course instructor permits and approves the registration, and

b. The lecture, lab and tutorial time table strictly permits, and

c. The student must have minimum CPI as 7.4.

F. Repeating a Course

1. When can a student repeat a course ?

A student must repeat a course in which he/she has obtained an F grade. Such course is regarded as a backlog course.

2. Do we have to repeat a backlog elective course ?

No, it is not necessary to repeat a backlog elective course. But the F grade will appear in Semester Grade Report and the Transcript. Student can replace the backlog elective course with the elective of same category (Open/Technical/Science).

3. Are there any conditions in order to be eligible to repeat a course for grade improvement ?

A student can repeat a course for grade improvement provided the following three conditions are satisfied:

a. Student must repeat a course in which a DD or DE grade was obtained, and

b. Student must have CPI less than 5.0 and must repeat a course in regular semester only, or

c. Student must have CPI less than 4.0 and must repeat a course in summer semester only.

4. Grade of which attempt will be considered in calculation of CPI ?

The grade obtained in the final attempt will be will be considered in calculation of CPI.

5. Will the grade of the first attempt be shown in the Transcript ?

Yes, the grade obtained in the first attempt will be shown in the Transcript, but will not be considered in calculation of CPI.

H. Award of Degree

1. What are minimum number of credits a student must complete in order to be eligible to get the degree ?

Students should complete minimum of 143 credits complete in order to be eligible to get the degree.

2. In which way these 143 credits must be completed ?

These 143 credits exclude the credits of following courses:

I. The Pass/Fail Courses -

  a. Introduction to Communication Skills PC-105 (2 credits)

  b. ICT for Freshers PC-108 (1 credit)

II. Rural Internship PC-232 (6 credits)

III. Summer Internship PC-333/PC-334 (9 credits

3. What is minimum CPI a student must have in order to be eligible to get the degree ?

Students should have minimum of 5.0 CPI in order to be eligible to get the degree. Thus, student must have minimum of 715 (143*5) grade points in order to be eligible to get the degree.

4. What are the other necessary conditions a student must satisfy in order being eligible to get the degree ?

A student can become eligible to get the degree provided the following three conditions are satisfied:

a. Student must have taken and passed all the core courses, and

b. Student must have passed all the foundation Pass/Fail courses prescribed in the curriculum, including all internships, and

c. Student must obtain a passing grade for the B. Tech Project.

d. Student should have registered for at least eight regular semesters (excluding the summer semesters) as regular student and should have paid all the institute fees.

I. Calculation of Final CPI

1. How will the final CPI calculated ?

The total credits from the coursework will be taken from at least number of courses which are required to get the 715 grade points; provided total credits must be at least 143 and must satisfy minimum requirements of different categories of electives courses.
